THE MANIPULATION FORMULA

Social cognitive theorists stated that television influences human behaviour in two ways, imitation and identification, these two patterns are product of the following interesting process:

Observational learning: Observers can aquire(learn) new behaviours simply by seeing those behaviours performed. Many of us who have never fired a handgun can do so because we've seen it done.

Inhibitory Effects: Seeing a model, a movie character, for example, punished for a behaviour reduces the likelihood that the observer will perform that behaviour. In the media we see good samaritans sued for trying to help someone and reduces our willingness to help in similar situations. That is inhibited by what we've seen.

Disinhibitory effects: Seeing a character rewarded for prohibited or threatning behaviour increases the likeihood that the observer will perform that behaviour, this is the basis for complaints against disney's megahit Moana and the glorification of crime and drugs in movies, for example. Behaviours that people will not adhere to, those that are inhibited now becomes more likely to occur.
